IntuiCAD SCM is considered a fundamental component to the design drafting profession by the American Design Drafting Association. IntuiCAD SCM and its related material is relevant to the delivery of the design drafting process.

... ADDA feels, it should be considered a key reference tool in furthering the knowledge, abilities, and skills of a properly trained designer or drafter in their pursuit of a professional career and job performance on a daily basis.

Our drawing component managers handle a variety of tasks from project assignment through turnkey and archive. While our product may be limited to AutoCad generated documents, our study was not and included administrative tasks like bidding, reports and quality control matters as well. Our drawing component managers target the broadest definition of the phrase, including:

 

  1. Borders
  2. Drawing stamps and logos
  3. Blocks and typical details
  4. Layer and drawing conversions
  5. Dimensions
  6. As-builts and archives

All of our component managers assume drawings will be used again and this "future use" is not only protected but enhanced at every opportunity.

 

Our extensive studies of CAD systems have revealed very low levels of variance in the final output and most common edits areas of study. While there were certainly differences of style and procedure, the intent and functionality aspects remained remarkably consistent. Based upon data from accumulated mark-ups, IntuiCAD addresses actual office practices under project conditions. The mark-ups represent actual work performed, the more complete the set of marks, the more measurable the job (and tool!) performance. While the comments may point to an aesthetic or style correction to be made, the fact remains that the correction was made.

Mark-ups also negate any need to discuss the source of errors. If the matter can be minimized through anticipatory or intuitive design it will cease to exist except for intentionally. Editing time for the vast majority of most common edits has been reduced 90% or more.

 

At IntuiCAD, efficient solutions mean out of the box production with an almost non-existent learning curve. Productive practices can be integrated into the drawings themselves, freeing engineers and designers to focus on far more crucial topics and tasks. Efficiency is capturing both ends of most common edits (the marking AND the editing). Most of all, at IntuiCAD efficiency comes from gathering the best from the best and making it available in a package that is as easily managed as an address book.
